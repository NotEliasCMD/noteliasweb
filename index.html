Agentic AI · Reliability

            
Agentic triage for Facebook's network

            
    
              
  • Spark
  • XGBoost
  • LangGraph
    • 
            

          

          

            
The problem

            
Facebook's network runs on live hardware emitting petabytes of telemetry a day, and when a link or box quietly degrades, latency spikes and services drop. Handling it ticket by ticket couldn't keep pace with growth — engineers drowned in false positives while the failures that mattered slipped through.

          

          

            
Approach

            
I started with petabyte-scale exploratory analysis of years of tickets and failure logs to find where the system actually bottlenecked. From there I trained tree-based models to predict machine health and triage incoming tickets, then wrapped them in agentic workflows that choose and carry out a fix — every action gated by real-engineer validation.

          

          

            
What I built

            
An end-to-end triage loop — predict, decide, act, verify — that resolves issues before they surface as outages.

            
    
              
  • Petabyte-scale EDA pipeline surfacing real failure modes
    • 
              
  • Tree-based models scoring machine health and triaging tickets
    • 
              
  • Human-validated agentic workflows that reroute and self-mitigate
    • 
            

          

          

            
Outcome

            
Monthly ticket volume dropped 40% as the system auto-resolved false positives and self-healed before impact, and the network held 98% uptime while it kept growing. Engineers were freed from repetitive triage to focus on the failures that genuinely needed them.

Hardware · Prices

            
RAM's AI premium

            
  • pandas
  • FRED
  • TrendForce

          

          

            TL;DRread the takeaways
            

              
    
              
  • After the 2022–23 glut, DRAM contract prices bottomed in 2023-Q3, then AI demand drove a ~4.2× reversal (index 68 → 289 by 2026-Q1).
    • 
              
  • The quarterly swing runs from −20% QoQ to +92% QoQ; the AI leg ignites in 2025-Q3 (+47.5%).
    • 
              
  • DDR5 16Gb spot prices nearly quadrupled in three months — $6.84 → $27.20 (+307%).
    • 
              
  • The driver is HBM: it sells at ~5× DDR5 and eats ~3× the wafer area per GB, starving the commodity DRAM market into shortage.
    • 
              
  • Official quality-adjusted indices barely register it — a story in itself.
    • 
            

            

          

          

            
From −20% to +92%: the quarterly swing

            
          

          

            
Why the official price indices miss it

            
          

          

            
The sharpest evidence: DDR5 spot

            
          

          

            
Method & honest caveats

            
TrendForce publishes quarterly DRAM contract-price change guidance (QoQ % ranges), not a public index, and no DRAM-specific series exists in FRED. The headline index is our own construction — the QoQ midpoints chained from a 2022-Q4 base — clearly labelled as illustrative, not an official price series; several 2025–26 quarters were revised sharply upward mid-quarter and we use the later figures. The FRED comparison (computer CPI, computer-storage PPI, retrieved 2026-07-12) is deliberately imperfect: neither is DRAM-specific, which is precisely why the cited TrendForce data is needed. Visualization-only, by design.
